Articulation: To put into words an idea or feeling of a specific type

Speech language pathologists frame articulation goals in terms of the child’s ability to learn. Literacy, math and writing skills may all fall in this area depending upon the extent to which articulation problems interfere with successful participation in learning activities, as well as whether or not the child communicates in such a way that the adults in the child’s life (i.e. teachers, child care providers, therapists) can accurately assess whether the child is learning and using age-expected preschool skills.

Fine & Gross Motor: Finger and hand skills such as writing

The ability to use large and small muscle groups that coordinate body movements involved in activities such as walking, running, jumping, throwing, writing, maintaining balance, buttoning, cutting, visual tracking, and tracing.

Self Care: Emotional hygiene

We address children’s ability to get dressed, toilet/bathe themselves, perform daily hygiene such as brushing teeth/combing hair, etc. We also address the skill sets and situations in which children perform entire routines; from getting ready for bed/ready in the morning to tolerating different transitions between play and work. We help children with their occupations and skills to eventually start helping to take care of them selves such as making a simple snack/meal to doing chores/homework successfully and efficiently.
